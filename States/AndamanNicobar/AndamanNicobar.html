Port Blair - Historical Capital

Heritage

Port Blair is home to the Cellular Jail, a symbol of India's fight for freedom, known for its historic significance and architectural grandeur.

Cuisine

Port Blair is known for its delicious seafood, especially the rich variety of fish, crabs, and lobsters prepared in a local style.

Festival

The Island Tourism Festival is a 10-day extravaganza featuring cultural performances, exhibitions, and food fairs, attracting visitors from all over.

Havelock Island - Nature's Paradise

Heritage

Havelock Island is famous for its pristine beaches, especially Radhanagar Beach, which is ranked among the best in Asia.

Cuisine

The island offers exquisite coconut-based dishes, showcasing the rich flavors of Andaman’s coastal cuisine.

Dance

The indigenous tribes of Andaman perform traditional dances during festivals, reflecting the rich cultural diversity of the island.

Neil Island - Tranquil Retreat

Heritage

Neil Island is known for its serene beaches and unspoiled natural beauty, offering a peaceful retreat for visitors.

Baratang Island - A Geological Wonder

Heritage

Baratang Island is famous for its Limestone Caves, Mud Volcanoes, and mangrove forests. It offers a glimpse into the geological wonders and the rich culture of the indigenous Jarawa Tribe.

Cuisine

Baratang Island offers freshly caught seafood like fish, prawns, and crabs. Grilled Fish and Fish Curry are local favorites, often cooked with coconut-based ingredients.

Festivals

While Baratang doesn’t have specific festivals, the Island Tourism Festival is celebrated across the Andaman region with local music, dance, and art displays.
